assessment

The site works. A few things stand out.


3.55 m ceilings give room for a full-height bespoke cabin without compression — important for heat stratification and the sense of volume inside. The former café use means plumbing is already roughed in for the wet room conversion, reducing build-out time and cost. Floor-to-ceiling glazing on the canal side gives the main room a clear orientation — guests face the water, the sauna is behind them. The layout naturally separates public and private zones.


Separate electricity meter means the M4 doesn't share load with the residential units above. That simplifies both the power budget and the billing. The 2017 build means modern wiring, insulation, and fire separation are already in place.


M4 makes sense here. A commercial spa needs consistent heat — the M4 runs 24/7 as the primary heating system, same wattage as a conventional electric heater, same heat output, but it also mines bitcoin. The net heating cost is lower and the site accumulates BTC on the balance sheet.

proposed installation

The main room (~70 m²) becomes the arrival space — lounge seating, cold plunge, and reception, all facing the canal through the full glass facade. The back room (~45 m²) houses the bespoke M1 sauna cabin with a changing corridor separating it from the main space. The M4 thermal module sits in the utility area, heating the building continuously. Existing WCs are retained; one is converted to a shower for post-sauna use.
