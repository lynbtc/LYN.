What the Basement Becomes

A bespoke indoor sauna cabin designed to the space. KOERNER-built, same construction standard as every numbered LYN unit — red cedar lining, ash benches, mineral wool insulation, aluminum foil vapor barrier. Fitted to the existing room geometry.

Because this is a basement installation, ventilation and moisture management are critical. The sauna needs 6–8 air changes per hour, and the surrounding basement space needs adequate dehumidification to prevent condensation issues in the subgrade envelope.

M4 thermal module

The Honest Assessment

The M4 replaces a conventional electric heater at the same wattage. All electrical energy in becomes heat energy out, and the unit also mines bitcoin. Same input, dual output. But the economics depend on two things: thermal demand and electricity price.

Germany has both the highest residential electricity price in mainland Europe and a strong heating season. These pull in opposite directions for M4.

Your Sauna Alone

At 4 sessions per week, ~1.5 hours each, the sauna needs roughly 7.7 kWh/day. An S19k Pro produces 66 kWh/day. That's a 12% thermal match. On its own, the sauna doesn't absorb enough heat.

With the House Connected

If the M4 heat routes into the house — floor heating, domestic hot water, or both — total thermal demand could reach 70–85 kWh/day. That would be a strong thermal match. But the electricity cost changes the equation.

The Comparison That Matters

A heat pump at COP 3.5 delivers the same heat for a fraction of the electricity.

At €0.35/kWh, the marginal cost of acquiring bitcoin through M4 is roughly 4× the market price. This is not a good M4 site on pure economics. Germany's electricity price is the single biggest obstacle.

What Would Need to Change

The M4 starts making sense at German sites when the electricity rate drops below ~€0.20/kWh. Some pathways to get there:

A solar PV system on the roof with direct self-consumption could reduce effective cost to €0.08–0.12/kWh for surplus generation. A time-of-use or dynamic tariff could offer lower rates during off-peak hours when the M4 runs. Or the German government's planned electricity tax reduction to the EU minimum could lower rates by €0.02–0.05/kWh if extended to households.

If the effective rate drops to €0.18/kWh, the same M4 unit would yield a marginal BTC acquisition cost of ~€85,000/BTC — still above market, but within range if you expect BTC appreciation. At €0.12/kWh (achievable with solar self-consumption), it drops to ~€32,000/BTC — well below market.

Economics based on current conditions (BTC ~€57k, difficulty 125.86 T, S19k Pro 120 TH/s, Feb 2026). We're being direct: at standard German residential rates, M4 doesn't justify itself on economics alone. If you have or plan solar PV, the picture changes — and we should model that scenario.

Open Questions

To move to the design phase for the indoor installation, we need some details about the basement space.

Basement dimensions and ceiling height?
Floor plan of the basement room where the sauna would go. Minimum 2,200 mm clear ceiling height for the cabin. Photos of the space are very helpful.
Is there floor drainage in the basement?
Indoor saunas need drainage for cleaning and condensation. If there's no existing drain, one can typically be added during buildout.
What's the electrical supply?
The KOERNER stove draws 6–9 kW. We need to know if 3-phase is available and what the panel capacity is. A typical German residential connection (3×35A or 3×50A) is usually sufficient.
How does the basement ventilate?
Sauna needs 6–8 air changes per hour. Fresh air in at floor level, exhaust out near the ceiling. Are there existing windows, light wells, or ducts that reach the exterior?
What's the current heating system in the house?
Gas boiler, oil, heat pump, district heating? And is it hydronic (water-based radiators or floor heating)? This matters for the M4 assessment — if solar PV is already installed or planned, we'd want to model M4 with self-consumption rates.
Do you have or plan solar PV?
A rooftop solar system with direct self-consumption is the most realistic path to M4 economics in Germany. If you already have one, share the system size (kWp) and your self-consumption rate — we'll model the M4 scenario.
