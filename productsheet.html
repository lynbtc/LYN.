Architecture.

Heater System

The stove and the thermal module.

Two kinds of heat. One system.

A sauna installation needs two kinds of heat. The sauna heater heats the cabin itself — it brings the room to sauna temperature (80-110 C) and holds infusion stones for steam. The heater is specified to the cabin volume during the design phase.

But the cabin sits inside a larger space. In a commercial building, the surrounding space needs continuous heating. In an outdoor installation, the changing room needs warmth and the cabin needs a warm baseline before the heater takes over. That ambient heat has to come from somewhere.

The M4 is that heat source. It mounts inside the M3 changing room locker shelving and replaces a conventional electric heater at the same wattage. All electrical energy in is converted to heat energy out.

The difference: it also mines bitcoin. Same electricity, same heat. Dual output.

Heater and M4
Sauna heater
Heats the sauna cabin to operating temperature. Finnish sauna, infusion stones, steam. Specified to cabin volume.
M4 thermal
Heats the space around the cabin. Changing room warmth, cabin pre-warming to ~20 C baseline, hot water routing where applicable. Runs continuously or seasonally.
Together
M4 holds the cabin at a warm baseline. The heater covers the delta to sauna temperature — not from ambient. In January without M4, the heater works from -5 C to 80 C. With M4, from 20 C to 80 C. Faster heat-up, better first session.

Site Application

Commercial indoor

Runs 24/7 as primary building heating system. Continuous thermal demand means high utilisation. The space needs heat regardless — electricity is a heating cost you pay anyway. Bitcoin is upside. Net heating cost is lower and the site accumulates BTC on its balance sheet.

Private outdoor

Runs seasonally — heating season only, approximately October through April. Heats the changing room and holds the cabin at ~20 C baseline. Without M4 the heater works from -5 C to 80 C. With M4, from 20 C to 80 C. Faster heat-up, comfortable changing room on arrival.

The M4 is optional. Configured per site based on thermal demand, electricity cost, and climate. The site planning tool evaluates each site individually.
